**You have a single treated unit (your website) and a long time series — that points strongly toward CausalImpact (Bayesian Structural Time Series).** But the quality of the estimate depends heavily on one thing:

> **Do you have any other time series that were *not* affected by the TV campaign?**

For example:
- Traffic from a channel the campaign didn't run on (e.g., direct traffic if the campaign only drove search)
- A competitor's traffic (if you have it)
- A related metric that TV ads wouldn't move (e.g., B2B sign-ups if the campaign targeted consumers)
- Traffic from a country where the campaign didn't air

**Why this matters**: Without a control series, CausalImpact builds a counterfactual purely from your own historical patterns — it models seasonality and trend from the pre-period and extrapolates. That works reasonably well with 2 years of data, but it can't distinguish "the campaign caused a lift" from "traffic was already trending up in March."

With a control series, the model uses that series as an anchor for what *would have happened*, which is much stronger identification.

---
