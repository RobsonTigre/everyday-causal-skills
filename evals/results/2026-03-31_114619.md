Based on what you've described, this is a **difference-in-differences (DiD)** problem — specifically **staggered DiD**, since stores were treated in three separate waves. With 500 stores and 2 years of weekly data, you have an excellent setup for this.

**Important nuance**: A naive two-way fixed effects (TWFE) regression can be biased here because it implicitly uses already-treated stores as controls for later-treated stores. With three cohorts, this can distort your estimates. You'll want **Callaway-Sant'Anna** (`did` package) or **Sun-Abraham** (`fixest` package) instead.
